## Pricing & Tone Direction (updated 2026-04-11)

**The product:** AI systems built specifically for propane operations — dispatch, customer service, call handling, quoting, knowledge. That's what they're buying and it should be front and center.

**The structure:** One person who has spent 10+ years in propane operations — who understands the industry from the inside — backed by a dedicated team of AI engineers and developers who build and maintain the systems. Clients get the domain expertise and personal accountability of someone who speaks their language, with the technical depth of a real team behind every implementation.

**The differentiator:** Austin is the relationship, the industry credibility, and the accountability. The team is what makes the implementations serious. When something goes wrong, they call Austin — not a ticket queue, not a support inbox. He knows who they are, he knows their operation, and he knows how to fix it.

**The tone:** Propane operators grew up in an industry built on personal accountability. A supplier shook your hand and kept his word — not because a contract forced it, but because that's how business worked. Ten75 should feel like that. The AI is modern; the relationship and accountability are old-school.

**"Fractional CAIO" — keep it, don't lead with it.** Useful as a descriptor of what the engagement looks like. Not the hook.

**On pricing:** Real cost exists — a retainer — but don't open with it. Let the relationship and accountability establish value first. When cost does come up (How We Work section, About page), frame it simply and let the guarantee do the trust work: "If the results aren't there, I keep working until they are."

### Voice reference points
- "Ten-plus years in propane operations. A team of AI engineers behind every implementation."
- "You deal with one person who knows this industry. A full technical team backs every system we build."
- "Not a freelancer guessing his way through your operation. Not a faceless agency that doesn't know propane from petroleum. Something built specifically for this."
- "When something goes sideways at 6am on a Monday in February, you call me. Not a help desk. Not a ticket. Me — and I know exactly what to do."
- "I've been in this industry long enough to know what operators are sick of: vendors who don't understand the business and disappear when things go wrong. We're built around the opposite of that."

## What APFX Does That Ten75 Should Borrow

1. **Punchy timing claims in the hero** — APFX leads with "Deployed in weeks, not quarters." Ten75 buries "First implementations live within 30 days" in step 3 of the process. That line should be in the hero.

2. **Explicit anti-consultant positioning** — APFX says "No drawn-out discovery phases padding the timeline" and "working deliverables over consulting theater." Ten75 gestures at this but pulls its punches.

3. **Operational credibility signals on About** — APFX has "ACCEPTING NEW CLIENTS / RESPONSE TIME: 24 HOURS" — small but powerful. Ten75's About page has none of this.

4. **Shorter sentences, everywhere** — Ten75 copy tends to 20-30 words per sentence. APFX averages 8-12. The ideas are strong — they just need tightening.

5. **Process section framing** — APFX names their method ("The APFX Method") and frames each step as a verb. Ten75's steps are good but don't have that "we've done this before" energy.

## Structural Ideas

### 1. Add a "Who This Is For" mini-section
APFX's targeting ("mid-market companies ready to go AI-native") is clear upfront. Ten75 could add a short section:

```
Section label: Right Fit

This is built for:
— Propane and fuel companies with 5–50 trucks
— Operations running manual processes they know can be better
— Owners and ops managers who want results, not reports
```

### 2. Condense the homepage
Ten75 has 8 sections. Consider collapsing Outcomes and Credibility into one tighter section — the metrics are great but somewhat redundant with what Use Cases already imply.

### 3. Services page intro
Add one line at the top of the services page:
```
"Every system below was designed for propane companies.
None of it came from a generic AI template."
```
